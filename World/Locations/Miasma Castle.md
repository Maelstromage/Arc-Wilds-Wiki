# Location: Miasma Castle

## Rumors
- Miasma Castle is a cursed stronghold wrapped in unnatural miasma.
- Those who die within the miasma rise again as undead.
- It is said that leaving the miasma can make a person deathly sick.
- Rumor claims the king within possesses a vorpal sword.
- The castle’s fall is tied to a beholder, a betrayal, and dark bargains.

## What is Known
- Miasma Castle is a cursed castle swallowed by miasma and rot.
- The miasma is central to the castle’s corruption.
- The dead within its influence may rise as undead.
- The castle is tied to King Cyril, Princess Cecilia, Ogmarth, Stewart, and Brottor Burrowfound.
- A beholder is bound up in the story of the castle’s downfall.
- The land around the castle became a swamp after the curse took hold.
- The place still holds royal chambers, barracks, jail cells, hidden rooms, and treasures from an older kingdom.

## History
- Miasma Castle was once a royal stronghold under King Cyril.
- Brottor Burrowfound was helping the king as an engineer and was involved in a mission concerning a lodestone for a flying ship.
- Stewart, the majordomo, brought a beholder into the kingdom.
- To deal with the beholder, Stewart made a bargain with hags.
- Instead of solving the problem, that bargain led to the miasma and the transformation of the surrounding land into a swamp.
- The castle is now a place of undead, broken loyalties, and unfinished royal business.

## People
### Ogmarth
- An undead figure who still retains his mind
- Knows much of the castle’s story

### Brottor Burrowfound
- Engineer tied to King Cyril and the flying-ship effort

### Princess Cecilia
- One of the central surviving cursed figures tied to the castle

### King Cyril
- Former ruler of the castle
- Still tied to the vorpal sword

### Stewart
- Majordomo whose actions helped doom the castle

# DM Notes
## Hidden Truths
- Stewart is Wart.
- Stewart brought the beholder into the kingdom and later made a deal with the hags to solve the problem.
- The deal created the miasma and the surrounding swamp instead.
- The beholder is now undead and locked in the throne room.
- King Cyril is now a spirit tied to the vorpal blade.
- Brottor is not dead.
- An airship lies beneath the king’s throne room.
- Lodestones naturally float and can be used for the airship.
- Three gems open the way to other temples.

## NPC Truths
- Ogmarth cannot safely leave; he believes he would die.
- Ogmarth believes destroying the beholder with the gems may allow the king to return.
- Cecilia can be uncursed, but doing so may kill her because her body is too far gone.
- Wart moves through the castle safely in ragged clothes.
- Riktor is a wererat.
- The king waits in his chambers to fight someone worthy of the vorpal blade.
- If someone defeats him under the right terms, he will give the sword away.
- Sword of Enki is sentient and wants everything done epically.

## Mechanics / Encounters
- Ogmarth has a blue gem and seeks the red and green gems.
- The red gem is behind a secret wall; the green gem’s location is unknown.
- The jail contains Brottor, Ricker Fink, Carter Burrat, and adventurers.
- Barracks were flushed with tar.
- There is a staircase to the cleric chambers.
- Key is under the bed in the king’s room.
- Needle trap in a box.
- Ceremonial weapons and paintings line parts of the royal area.
- Statues can be restored to human form with a potion or scroll of greater restoration.
- The wizard and priest may have been turned to stone by the beholder.
- There are blessed daggers in wizard chests so the dead do not rise again.
- Remove Curse can fix the miasma condition.
- There is a map with temples on it.
- Potion of flying, an enduring spellbook, a pass without trace necklace, and other treasures are present.
- Three turns of waiting plus reducing the king’s hit points under the proper conditions can win the blade.
- A siege engine may be able to barrel through the castle.
- There are 3 random commoner escort NPCs in one thread.

- The sentient Sword of Enki
- The surviving prisoners and what they do next
